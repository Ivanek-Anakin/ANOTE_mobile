Patient Michael Brown, 45 years old, presents with productive cough, fever, and shortness of breath for 4 days. Onset was sudden after attending a conference. Fever up to 38.8°C at home, chills, fatigue. Non-smoker, no chronic lung disease. No known allergies. On examination: temperature 38.3°C, oxygen saturation 95%, decreased breath sounds and crackles at right base on auscultation. Diagnosis: community-acquired pneumonia, right lower lobe. Plan: amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125mg twice daily for 7 days, rest, adequate hydration, follow-up in 5 days or sooner if symptoms worsen.
